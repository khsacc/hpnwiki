# MITO system

**Reference:**
Komatsu, K., Moriyama, M., Koizumi, T., Nakayama, K., Kagi, H., Abe, J., & Harjo, S. (2013). **Development of a new P–T controlling system for neutron-scattering experiments.** High Pressure Research, 33(1), 208-213. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/08957959.2012.762914


'MITO system' is a pressure-temperature (*p*-*T*) variable system, particularly designed for neutron scattering experiments. The MITO system allows us

1. to control *p* up to 10 GPa, and *T* from 80 K to 473 K
2. to control *p* even at 80 K
3. to control *T* with very high rate (up to ~20 K/min)
4. to control *T* stably (± 0.1 K over 100 K, and ± 0.3 K below 100 K, approximately)
5. to download *p* to 0 GPa even at 80 K. *(it is not straight forward for other systems)*

The above characteristics of MITO system are achieved by the following contrivances, those identify the MITO system to other similar systems.

- The thermally insulated anvils using zirconia and GFRP seats. (contributes to 1, 2, 3, 5)
- Flow liquid-nitrogen by evacuating (not by compressing) nitrogen gas after through the cell and the flow-rate is precisely controlled by mass flow controller. (contributes to 4)

Please see our paper to find more details.

Komatsu, K., Moriyama, M., Koizumi, T., Nakayama, K., Kagi, H., Abe, J., and Harjo, S. (2013) Development of a new P–T controlling system for neutron-scattering experiments. *High Pressure Research*, 33(1), 208–213. https://doi.org/10.1080/08957959.2012.762914

## Acknowledgements

The basic idea — flowing liquid nitrogen around anvils with thermal insulator — was inspired from the similar system in ISIS (introduced in '[Techniques in High Pressure Neutron Scattering](https://www.amazon.co.jp/Techniques-High-Pressure-Neutron-Scattering/dp/1439835624)'), when Komatsu was visiting at the Univ. of Edinburgh as a post-doc from 2006 to 2009 to collaborate with Dr. Craig Bull, Dr. John Loveday and Prof. Richard Nelmes. The 'MITO system' was named by Dr. Stefan Klotz, because this system was first presented in the international conference held in Mito-city, Japan ('IUCr Commission on High Pressure 2012 Meeting "Advances in Crystallography at High Pressure" / International Symposium of the Quantum Beam Science Directorate, Japan Atomic Energy Agency', Sep 23–27, 2012, in Mito, Japan).

The project had been financially supported by Grants-in-Aid for Creative Scientific Research for 'Material Sciences at Ultra-High Pressure using the Strongest Spallation Neutron Source' (19GS0205), the Global COE Program for Deep Earth Mineralogy from the Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT), and the MEXT Grant No. 21840024.
